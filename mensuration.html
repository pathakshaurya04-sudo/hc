Reproductive Wellness (Menstrual Period)

Navigating Your Cycle with Confidence

Your menstrual cycle is a vital sign—a unique rhythm that tells a story about your overall health. We’re here to help you decode those signals, turning monthly transitions into opportunities for balance, energy, and proactive self-care.

1. Introduction to Menstrual Health

Menstruation, commonly known as a period, is a natural monthly process in which the body sheds the lining of the uterus. It is an important part of reproductive health and serves as a vital indicator of your overall well-being.

2. What is a Menstrual Cycle?

The cycle is a sophisticated hormonal journey that prepares the body for potential pregnancy. While every body is unique, a healthy cycle generally follows these parameters:

  • Cycle Length: Usually 21–31 days.
  • Bleeding Phase: Typically lasts 3–5 days.

4. Menstrual Hygiene

  • Product Variety: Use sanitary pads, tampons, or menstrual cups based on your activity level.
  • The 4-6 Hour Rule: Change pads/tampons frequently to maintain optimal freshness.
  • Sanitization: Always wash hands before and after changing products.

The "4-Hour Rule" & RTI Prevention

A key public health standard: Changing sanitary products every 4 to 6 hours is critical to preventing **Reproductive Tract Infections (RTI)** and Toxic Shock Syndrome. Even with a light flow, bacteria can proliferate in moist environments; frequent changes maintain the natural pH balance of your body.

Medical Consultation Red Flags

If you encounter any of the following symptoms, it may indicate underlying conditions such as PCOS, Endometriosis, or hormonal deficiencies. Early diagnosis is the key to effective management.

  • Menorrhagia: Soaking through a pad every hour for several hours.
  • Amenorrhea: Absence of periods for 3+ months (excluding pregnancy).
  • Severe Pelvic Pain: Pain that prevents daily activity or work.
  • Intermenstrual Bleeding: Spotting or bleeding between cycles.

Your Monthly Wellness Roadmap

01

Track & Observe

Use a digital tracker or journal to note your start date, energy levels, and mood. Patterns emerge after 3 months.

02

Choose Your Fit

Select products based on activity: Pads for comfort, Tampons for swimming, or Menstrual Cups for 12hr protection.

03

The Clean Slate

Maintain the 4-hour rule. Use unscented, pH-balanced cleansers for the external area only to protect natural flora.

04

Cycle Syncing

Adjust your diet: Increase Iron in the Menstrual phase and Complex Carbs in the Luteal phase to stabilize mood.
