Nutrition for Two

Proper nutrition is the cornerstone of fetal brain development and maternal energy. Focus on a "rainbow plate" approach to ensure a full spectrum of vitamins.

  • DHA & Omega-3: Essential for baby's eye and brain growth.
  • Hydration: Aim for 3 Liters of water to support amniotic fluid.

Pregnancy Common Concerns

Is exercise safe during the first trimester?

Generally, yes. Low-impact activities like prenatal yoga and walking are encouraged unless your doctor advises otherwise due to high-risk factors.

When should I feel the first kick?

Commonly referred to as "quickening," most mothers feel movement between weeks 18 and 22. First-time moms might feel it slightly later.

What are the "Red Flags" to watch for?

Sudden swelling of face/hands, blurred vision, or decreased fetal movement should be reported to your consultant immediately.
